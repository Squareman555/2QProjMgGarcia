Who are the Midlaners?

Midlaners are the ones who are assigned to go to the middle of the map. Their primary job is to farm, and visit other lanes if necessary.

The champions who are played in this lane are usually waek early (i.e. mages), but scale up and stack as the game progresses. Some examples include: Kayle (gets power spikes at levels 6, 11, and 16), Veigar (his passive stacks AP), Kassadin (his E stacks when abilities are triggered near him), among others. The mid lane is a good fit for these champions because the small size of the lane allows for easier escapes to safety.

Map Awareness

This skill is a must-know for all players, especially midlaners. It's very important that they get used to look at the map every once in a while to look at their minimap since they are in the middle of the map. They need to watch out for ganks from above and below them, as well as keep a close watch to other lanes to see if they'll be needing a gank or not.

One can learn to master this skill by reminding themself to look at the map, or if possible have someone do it for you. This skill is what separates experienced players from beginners. Always look at your minimaps, kids!

Ganks

This is where map awareness comes into play. Ganking is the act of visitting other lanes to help them take out the enemies, or to rescue allies from being killed by the enemies.

Sometimes, you don't need to wait for someone to request a gank. If you see that the enemy in another lane has no wards placed around their lane, therefore making them vulnerable to ganks, you can teleport or sneak your way into that lane and jump for a kill or two.

Before you leave your lane to gank another, shove your minion wave into the enemy turret, so when you return, you'll have a big, fat, and juicy enemy minion wave waiting for you to harvest them.
